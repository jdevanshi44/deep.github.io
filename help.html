About Sensors

Temperature

    • Temperature is a measure of degree of hotness or coldness of an object or substances.
    • Temperature is an important factor in everyday life, as it affects our comfort and well-being.
    • It affects the behavior of materials and substances including their physical properties, chemical reactions and biological properties.
    • Normal drinking water, the temperature ranges between 0℃ (32℉) and 20℃ (68℉).
    • Usually, there is no growth above 55℃, and a temperature of over 60℃ has bactericidal effect. Thus, WHO recommends that water be heated and stored at 60℃.

Turbidity

    • Turbidity is a measure of the cloudiness or haziness of a liquid caused by presence of suspended particles.
    • Turbidity can be caused by a variety of factors such as sediment, algae, organic matters or inorganic particles.
    • It’s commonly measured in units of Nephelometric Turbidity Units (NTU) using devices called as turbidity sensor. Through this sensor we can identify the number of suspended particles in water.
    • Higher turbidity levels can interfere with disinfection processes and can also indicate presences of pathogens or contamination in water.
    • The turbidity level should be below 1 NTU according to World Health Organization (WHO).

Conductivity

    • Conductivity is a measure of a material’s ability to conduct electricity.
    • The conductivity of material depends on various factors such as presence of impurities.
    • The unit of conductivity is siemens per meter(S/m) or mho per meter (mho/m).
    • Conductivity is an important factor for determining the quality of water.
    • High conductivity can indicate the presence of dissolved salts and other impurities.
    • Normal drinking water, the conductivity ranges between 50 to 1500 micro siemens per centimeter (µS/cm).

pH

    • pH is a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, such as water.
    • The pH scales from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral.
    • pH value below 7 indicates acidity, while a pH value above 7 indicates alkalinity.
    • pH is an important factor for normal drinking water.
    • Normal drinking water, pH ranges between 6.5 to 8.5 according to WHO.
    • Water utilities may adjust the acceptable range and is safe for consumption.
    • If the pH of drinking water is too low or too high, it can affect the taste and appearance of water as well as potentially impact the safety of the water.

Water Level

    • Water level refers to the height or depth of water in a particular location.
    • Water level data is often collected and analyzed over time to identify trends and patterns, as well as to inform water management systems.
    • The unit of conductivity is siemens per meter(S/m) or mho per meter (mho/m).
    • Water level is an important parameter to monitor for many reasons.
    • Ranges depends on the size of the overhead / under head tanks.
